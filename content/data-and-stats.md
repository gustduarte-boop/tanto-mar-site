# Real Data & Stats for the Site

## The Founding Paper — Directly About Corumbau

**Duarte, G.A.S., Villela, H.D.M., et al. (2020). Heat Waves Are a Major Threat to Turbid Coral Reefs in Brazil. *Frontiers in Marine Science*, 7:179.**
DOI: 10.3389/fmars.2020.00179

*Gustavo Duarte is first author. This paper directly documents the collapse that motivated founding Tanto Mar.*

### Key verified numbers — use these on the site:

- **~86% mortality of *Millepora alcicornis*** at Pedra do Silva and Virada de Fora reefs — inside the Marine Extractive Reserve of Corumbau (MERC)
- **~43% mortality** at Coroa Vermelha reef (Santa Cruz de Cabrália)
- **First mass die-off ever recorded in South Atlantic reefs** — unprecedented in the region's history
- **DHW of 19.65** — highest Degree Heating Weeks ever recorded at the Abrolhos reef station (May 10, 2019), almost doubling the previous record of 10.95 (2016)
- **114 days above the bleaching threshold (27.9°C)** in 2019 alone — vs. only 250 days in the entire 2010–2018 period
- **50 consecutive days with DHW above 15** (April 12 – May 31, 2019)
- **2019: highest single-day temperature recorded since 1989**
- **Itacolomis Reef Complex (Corumbau RESEX): ~50 km²** — the largest reef complex in the Marine Extractive Reserve of Corumbau
- **Live coral cover at study sites**: 8.5–14.5% (Corumbau reefs, 2019)
- ***Millepora alcicornis* is a branching hydrocoral and one of the major reef-building species** in the Western South Atlantic — its loss restructures the entire reef ecosystem

### What this means for the site narrative:
The "A Última Miléporas Viva" video was filmed at this moment of collapse. The biobank initiative exists because *M. alcicornis* — the only branching coral in the South Atlantic — nearly disappeared from Corumbau in a single summer. Without genetic preservation, the next heatwave could mean permanent loss.

## Supporting Paper — Regional Context

**Teixeira, C.D. et al. (2019). Sustained mass coral bleaching (2016–2017) in Brazilian turbid-zone reefs. *Coral Reefs*.**
DOI: 10.1007/s00338-019-01789-6

- **2016–2017: one of the longest sustained mass bleaching episodes on record** (12+ months)
- **10 coral species bleached simultaneously** — unprecedented for Abrolhos
- **Fire corals (Millepora spp.) most affected** — >40% colonies bleached
- **Abrolhos Bank**: >8,000 km² — largest reef complex in the South Atlantic
- **5 mass bleaching episodes** in Western South Atlantic since 1993
- **<3% total coral mortality** in 2016–2017 — but 2019 changed everything (Duarte et al. 2020)
